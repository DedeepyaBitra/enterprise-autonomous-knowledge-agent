# NovaTech Solutions — Employee Handbook

## 1. Company Overview

NovaTech Solutions is an enterprise AI software company founded in 2018 and headquartered in Bangalore, India, with a secondary office in Pune and a distributed remote workforce across India, Singapore, and the United States. NovaTech builds AI workflow automation products, including autonomous agents, RAG-based enterprise search, and CRM/GTM automation tooling for mid-market and enterprise customers.

NovaTech employs approximately 640 people across Engineering, AI Research, Product, Design, Sales, Marketing, Customer Success, Finance, HR, and IT Operations.

## 2. Mission & Vision

**Mission:** To democratize enterprise AI by building intelligent automation systems that let every organization operate at the speed of thought.

**Vision:** To be the most trusted autonomous AI workflow partner for enterprises worldwide, known for reliability, transparency, and measurable business impact.

**Core Values:**

2. Ownership — treat company problems as personal problems.
3. Transparent Communication — default to open information sharing.

## 3. Working Hours

- Standard working hours: **9:30 AM to 6:30 PM, Monday to Friday** (9 hours including a 1-hour lunch break).
- Core collaboration hours (mandatory availability): **11:00 AM to 4:00 PM IST**.
- Office-based employees follow a **hybrid model**: minimum 3 days in-office per week (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday default anchor days), 2 days flexible (office or WFH).
- Fully remote-designated roles (approved by VP+) are exempt from in-office days but must honor core collaboration hours.
- Weekly working hours: **40 net hours** (5 days x 8 net hours/day, i.e., the 9:30 AM-6:30 PM span minus the 1-hour daily lunch break). The 45-hour figure sometimes referenced elsewhere is the **gross** weekly office-hours span (9 hours/day x 5 days) and *includes* lunch breaks — use 40 hours for any net-working-hours calculation.

## 4. Attendance Policy

- Attendance is logged via the NovaTech HRMS portal (biometric for office days, self-check-in for WFH days).
- Employees must check in within the grace period defined in the HR & Leave Policy (Section: Late Coming Policy).
- 3 consecutive unexplained absences without approved leave or manager communication are treated as job abandonment and escalated to HR for disciplinary review.
- Attendance records are reviewed monthly by managers and HR Business Partners (HRBPs).

## 5. Employee Code of Conduct

- Employees must act with integrity, honesty, and professionalism in all interactions, internal and external.
- Zero tolerance for harassment, discrimination, or retaliation based on gender, religion, caste, disability, sexual orientation, or any protected characteristic.
- Conflicts of interest (e.g., outside employment, financial interest in vendors/competitors) must be disclosed to HR in writing.
- Confidential company information, customer data, and source code must not be shared externally without written authorization.
- Use of company assets (laptops, software licenses, cloud credits) is restricted to business purposes; incidental personal use is permitted within reasonable limits.
- Violations are handled via a three-stage disciplinary process: **Verbal Warning → Written Warning → Termination**, except in cases of gross misconduct (fraud, harassment, data theft), which may result in immediate termination.

## 6. Communication Guidelines

- Primary communication tools: Slack (real-time), Email (formal/external), Jira (task tracking), Confluence (documentation).
- Slack response expectation: within 4 business hours during core hours.
- Email response expectation: within 1 business day.
- All customer-facing communication must be reviewed for tone and accuracy before sending; Customer Success and Sales use approved templates for sensitive topics (refunds, security incidents, SLA breaches).
- Company-wide announcements are sent via email and posted in the #announcements Slack channel.
- Security or compliance incidents must be reported immediately to security@novatechsolutions.com and cc'd to the employee's manager.

## 8. Onboarding Process

1. **Pre-boarding (Day -7 to Day -1):** Offer letter signed, background verification initiated, laptop and accounts provisioned by IT Operations based on role.
2. **Day 1:** HR orientation session, handbook walkthrough, policy acknowledgment sign-off, buddy assignment.
3. **Week 1:** IT setup validation (VPN, email, Slack, Jira, GitHub/Figma access per role), department-specific onboarding with manager, 30-60-90 day goal setting.
4. **Day 30:** Check-in with HRBP and manager on onboarding experience.
5. **Day 90:** Interim probation review meeting (see Performance Review Process) — a progress check-in; employment continues if progress is satisfactory.
6. **Day 180:** Final probation review (see Performance Review Process). Confirmation letter issued if satisfactory, marking the end of the probation period.

Standard probation period is **6 months** from date of joining for all full-time employees.

## 9. Offboarding Process

1. Resignation submitted in writing via HRMS portal with notice period as per HR & Leave Policy.
2. Manager and HR conduct a **retention conversation** within 3 business days (optional, not mandatory to accept resignation).
3. Knowledge transfer plan created and executed during notice period.
4. Exit interview conducted by HR (in-person or virtual) in the final week.
5. IT Operations initiates **access revocation checklist**: email deactivation, VPN revocation, GitHub/Jira/Figma/OpenAI API access removal, laptop and asset return.
6. Full and Final (F&F) settlement processed within 45 days of last working day, including pending leave encashment (up to 15 days), reimbursements, and deductions.
7. Relieving letter and experience certificate issued upon completion of asset return and clearance.

## 10. Performance Review Process

- **Probation Review:** At Day 90 and Day 180, evaluated by manager against onboarding goals.
- **Bi-Annual Performance Review Cycle:** April–September (H1) and October–March (H2).
- Review components: Self-assessment, Manager assessment, Peer feedback (360, for Senior+ roles), Goal (OKR) attainment score.
- Rating scale: **Exceeds Expectations, Meets Expectations, Needs Improvement, Unsatisfactory.**
- "Needs Improvement" ratings trigger a **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)** of 60 days, jointly defined by manager and HRBP.
- Two consecutive "Unsatisfactory" ratings or a failed PIP may lead to termination, subject to HR review.
- Promotions and bonuses are determined at the end of each bi-annual cycle (see HR & Leave Policy — Promotion Policy).
